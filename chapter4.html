Important Questions - Chapter 4
Moving Charges and Magnetism

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

  • The unit of magnetic field (B) is:
    Answer: Tesla (T)
  • Magnetic force on a moving charge is maximum when the angle between velocity and magnetic field is:
    Answer: 90°
  • What is the direction of magnetic force on a positive charge moving in a magnetic field?
    Answer: Given by right-hand rule
  • Which law gives the magnetic field due to a current element?
    Answer: Biot–Savart Law
  • Which force is responsible for circular motion of a charged particle in magnetic field?
    Answer: Magnetic Lorentz Force

Very Short Questions (VSQs)

  • Define magnetic field.
    Answer: The region around a magnet or current-carrying conductor where its magnetic effect can be felt.
  • State Biot–Savart Law.
    Answer: dB = (μ₀/4π) × (Idl × r̂)/r²
  • What is Lorentz force?
    Answer: F = q(E + v × B), the total force on a charged particle due to electric and magnetic fields.
  • What is the expression of magnetic force on a current-carrying wire?
    Answer: F = I (L × B)
  • What is the SI unit of magnetic field?
    Answer: Tesla (T)

Long Answer Questions

  • Derive the expression for magnetic force on a moving charge.
    Answer: F = q(v × B). It is maximum when v is perpendicular to B and zero when v is parallel to B.
  • Explain the motion of a charged particle in a uniform magnetic field.
    Answer: The particle moves in a circular path if the velocity is perpendicular to B. Radius r = mv/qB.
  • State and explain Ampere’s Circuital Law.
    Answer: ∮B·dl = μ₀I. The line integral of magnetic field around a closed loop is proportional to the total current enclosed.
  • Derive the magnetic field at the center of a circular loop carrying current I.
    Answer: B = (μ₀I)/(2R), where R is the radius of the loop.
  • Derive the expression for magnetic field on the axis of a circular current loop.
    Answer: B = (μ₀IR²) / [2(R² + x²)^(3/2)], where x is the distance from center along axis.
