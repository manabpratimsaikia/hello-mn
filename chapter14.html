Important Questions - Chapter 14
Semiconductor Electronics

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

  • Which material is commonly used in semiconductors?
    Answer: Silicon
  • What type of semiconductor is obtained by doping silicon with phosphorus?
    Answer: n-type
  • In a forward-biased diode, current flows due to:
    Answer: Majority charge carriers
  • Which logic gate gives a high output only when all inputs are high?
    Answer: AND gate
  • The reverse biasing of a diode:
    Answer: Increases the width of depletion layer

Very Short Questions (VSQs)

  • What is a semiconductor?
    Answer: A material whose conductivity lies between conductors and insulators.
  • Name two intrinsic semiconductors.
    Answer: Silicon and Germanium
  • What is doping?
    Answer: The process of adding impurities to a semiconductor to increase conductivity.
  • What is the function of a diode?
    Answer: It allows current to flow in one direction only.
  • Write the Boolean expression for an OR gate.
    Answer: Y = A + B

Long Answer Questions

  • Explain the energy band diagram of conductors, insulators, and semiconductors.
    Answer: In conductors, valence and conduction bands overlap; in insulators, a large band gap exists; in semiconductors, the gap is small allowing electron excitation.
  • Distinguish between n-type and p-type semiconductors.
    Answer: n-type has electrons as majority carriers (doped with group 15), p-type has holes (doped with group 13).
  • Explain the working of a p-n junction diode in forward and reverse bias.
    Answer: In forward bias, depletion layer narrows and current flows; in reverse bias, depletion layer widens and current is negligible.
  • Explain the working of a transistor in common emitter configuration.
    Answer: In CE mode, it provides voltage and current gain; input is at base-emitter, output is at collector-emitter.
